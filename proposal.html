UW Campus Cops Proposal

Problem Description

The topic domain for our project focuses on campus safety concerns for students at the University of Washington (UW) Seattle campus. Campus safety is a critical issue that impacts the well-being and security of students, faculty, and staff. With a large and bustling urban campus environment, UW Seattle faces various safety challenges, including but not limited to theft, assault, harassment, and navigating poorly lit or unfamiliar areas at night. These concerns not only affect individual safety but also influence the overall campus atmosphere and student experience.

One information technology problem in this space is the lack of a comprehensive and user-friendly tool that provides real-time safety information, resources, and assistance tailored to the UW-Seattle community. While there are existing campus safety resources such as blue light emergency phones, campus police services, and safety escort programs accessing and navigating these resources efficiently can be troublesome and may not always align with the diverse needs of students. In terms of previous attempts to address campus safety through technology, the SafeCampus app offers features such as emergency contacts, reporting options, and safety tips, but it may still face challenges in meeting the diverse needs of students. Issues such as user awareness, app usability, and the comprehensiveness of safety resources could impact the app's effectiveness in addressing campus safety concerns.

Resources related to our topic:

  • UW Police - Annual Security and Fire Safety Reports
  • UW Police - Safety Escort Services
  • SafeCampus

Proposed Application

The proposed web app aims to address campus safety concerns for students, faculty, and staff at the University of Washington (UW) Seattle campus. It will provide a comprehensive and user-friendly platform offering real-time safety information, resources, and assistance tailored to the UW-Seattle community.

Users:

  • Students, faculty, and staff of the UW Seattle campus will be the primary users of the application.
  • The app will particularly cater to minority groups within the campus community, ensuring their safety concerns are adequately addressed.

Features:

  1. Real-time Safety Alerts: Users can receive push notifications or check the app for real-time updates on safety incidents, including theft, assault, harassment, and other campus safety concerns.
  2. Interactive Safety Map: The app will feature an interactive map highlighting well-lit areas, emergency blue light phone locations, safe routes, and areas with reported safety incidents.
  3. Personal Safety Plan Generator: Users can input their class schedules, routes, and preferences to generate personalized safety plans, including suggested safe routes, recommended escort services, and emergency contacts.

How It Solves the Problem:

By consolidating real-time safety information, resources, and assistance in one user-friendly platform, the app streamlines access to campus safety resources, enhancing overall safety awareness and preparedness within the UW-Seattle community.

Tailored features such as personalized safety plans and interactive maps address diverse user needs, ensuring that minority groups feel represented and supported in their safety concerns.

App Proposal Overview

The proposed web application, "UW Campus Cops," aims to enhance campus safety and security for the University of Washington (UW) Seattle community.

Users and Impact

The primary users of the UW Campus Cops app will be the students, faculty, and staff of UW Seattle.

Features and Interaction

The UW Campus Cops app will include "two and a half" significant interactive features:

  • Real-Time Safety Map: A dynamic map displaying real-time safety alerts, location of blue light emergency phones.
  • Emergency Resource Directory: A comprehensive list of emergency contacts, safety escort services.
  • Safety Check-In and Escort Request: Users can check in to notify friends or family of their safe arrival.

Data and Resources

The app will utilize data from:

  • UW Police for real-time safety alerts and reports.

Solving the Campus Safety Problem

The UW Campus Cops app addresses campus safety concerns by providing a centralized, user-friendly platform.
